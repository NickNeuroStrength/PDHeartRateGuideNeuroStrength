Why a different formula for Parkinson's? In 82 people with mild-to-moderate PD completing a maximal cardiopulmonary exercise test (CPET), the standard 220−age formula overestimated true max HR in 88% of participants. The regression-based estimator below had 63% lower mean square error than 220−age — because it accounts for both age and resting heart rate, making it more individualized and safer for HIT programming.

Rosenfeldt AB, Penko AL, Jansen AE, Lopez-Lennon C, Zimmerman E, Imrey PB, Singh TK, Dibble LE, Alberts JL. "Refining Maximal Heart Rate Estimation to Enhance Exercise Recommendations for Persons With Parkinson Disease." Arch Phys Med Rehabil. 2025 Nov;106(11):1680–1684. doi: 10.1016/j.apmr.2025.03.046.
